Who We Are

The Agency Built for Whitley County’s Markets.

Whitley County is one of Northeast Indiana’s most underserved digital marketing markets. Columbia City functions as a genuine regional commercial hub, pulling customers from across the county for home services, dining, retail, medical, and professional services. Its position directly along US-30—one of Indiana’s primary east-west commercial corridors—delivers consistent traffic from outside the county into Columbia City’s business district. Most Columbia City businesses are operating with outdated websites and no coherent digital strategy. The first to build real visibility will own their categories for years.

Whitley County’s Economy. Agricultural Roots, US-30 Commerce, Growing Residential Demand.
Whitley County’s economy blends a deep agricultural tradition with an active commercial corridor along US-30 and a residential community that increasingly expects the same digital accessibility found in larger Indiana markets. Churubusco’s lake-country identity brings seasonal energy. Larwill and South Whitley serve rural and semi-rural customers who use Google to find contractors and services they cannot find nearby. The gap between what Whitley County customers expect digitally and what most local businesses offer is substantial. That gap is opportunity.

Outside Columbia City, the county’s character is shaped by tight-knit rural and small-town communities. Churubusco, in the county’s northwest, is known for its Turtle Days festival and a community identity built around local pride and outdoor recreation. Larwill, along the US-30 corridor, is a small crossroads community with agricultural and home services demand and essentially no local digital competition. South Whitley, on the Whitley-Kosciusko county line, draws customers from both counties and sits adjacent to the Kosciusko lake-country market without the digital competition that lake-country markets to the east have already developed.

Favze is based in Middlebury in Elkhart County, operating across the same Northern Indiana market network that connects directly to Whitley County through the US-30 corridor and shared regional economic patterns.
